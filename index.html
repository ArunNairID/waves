Have you ever been on a freeway, and suddenly traffic comes to a stop for no visible reason? Or have you ever been stuck in bumper-to-bumper congestion, and suddenly traffic clears up? If so, then you've experienced a traffic wave.

A traffic wave is a block of slow-moving vehicles on a freeway that travels backwards over time. If a road is crowded with vehicles, then just one vehicle slowing down a little bit can set off a traffic wave.

Click "Hit the Brakes" to slow down the highlighted car, and wait until the traffic wave forms. Mouse over a car to slow down time and see its velocty and acceleration. Clicking on a car makes it the one that brakes when you press the button.

What causes traffic waves?

They happen because of two facts about driving:

  1. Cars take longer to speed up than to slow down, so if you brake suddenly then it will take you a while to reach your original speed.
  2. People have reaction times...if the car in front of you slows down all of a sudden, you don't slow down until a couple seconds later. And the more time you lose reacting, the harder you have to brake to keep a safe distance.

Therefore, on a crowded road, if one car brakes, then the car behind it has to brake harder, and the car behind that one has to brake even harder, and so on.
