The equation says, "At time , you accelerate at a rate proportional to the difference in speed between your car and the speed of the car you're following, but with a gap of seconds." So if you're going faster than the car in front of you, then you slow down. And if you're going slower, you speed up.

With time, researchers have developed more and more complex models of traffic behavior that add more realism or agree more closely with data from traffic detectors. For example, our simple equation assumes that the car in front of us affects our behavior even if it's a mile away. Some of the first improvements on the equation added terms for the size of the gap and the fact cars can slow down much more quickly than they can speed up.
